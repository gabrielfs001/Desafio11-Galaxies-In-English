The largest Galaxies

And curiosities about them

IC 1101

The IC 1101 is the biggest galaxy in the universe. The galaxy is elliptical to lenticular and appears with golden yellow coloring. The brightest galaxy is located more than 1.04 billion light-years from Earth.

Phoenix Cluster

Phoenix Cluster is one of the most solid groups in the universe. It's located around 5.7 billion light-years from Earth. The elliptical galaxy is composed of average and dark intra-group matter.

NGC 262

The NGC 262 is located 202 million light-years from Earth, which is one of the largest known spiral galaxies. The diameter of the galaxy is approximately 1.3 million light-years.

NGC 4889

The NGC 4889, also famous known as “Coma B”, is a supergiant elliptical galaxy located approximately 308 million light-years from Earth. It was also discovered by the British astronomer Frederick William Herschel I in 1785.
